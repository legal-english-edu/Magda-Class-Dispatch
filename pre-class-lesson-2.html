This week's topic

When the state looks away

In January 2024, a joint investigation by Le Monde and Radio France revealed that Nestlé had been illegally treating its mineral water — Perrier, Vittel, Contrex, Hépar — using filtration methods banned under French law. The treatments, which included UV light and activated carbon filters, were used to mask bacterial contamination and pesticide residues. The water being sold as pure and natural was neither.

What made the story more than a corporate scandal was what followed. The French government had known since August 2021. Rather than act, it covered up. The cover-up reached the Élysée Palace. For two and a half years, officials at the highest levels of the French state chose to protect a multinational company over the public it was supposed to serve.

A French Senate inquiry confirmed the cover-up in May 2025. Paris headquarters were raided in July 2025. Nestlé paid a fine of €2 million — on a fraud estimated at over €3 billion. The head of production at Nestlé Waters was referred to the Paris prosecutor on suspicion of perjury.

The case raises questions that go beyond food safety. It asks what corporate crime actually is, how it is prosecuted — or not — and where the line falls between a company breaking the law and a state that helps it do so.

For Thursday

Discussion questions

01
Nestlé paid €2 million to avoid prosecution on a €3 billion fraud. What does that tell you about how the law handles corporations differently from individuals?
02
The French state was complicit from the beginning — it knew and covered up. Where does corporate crime end and state crime begin?
Vocabulary

Words for this week

cover-up
zatajenie, ukrycie prawdy
The concealment of a mistake or crime, especially by those in authority.
The Senate report confirmed that what had appeared to be negligence was in fact a deliberate cover-up.
inquiry
dochodzenie, postępowanie wyjaśniające
A formal investigation, especially by a government body.
The parliamentary inquiry heard testimony from senior officials who had been present at the original briefings.
collusion
zmowa, działanie w porozumieniu
Secret cooperation between parties for a fraudulent or illegal purpose.
Prosecutors argued that the pattern of behaviour pointed not to negligence but to collusion between the regulator and the company.
regulatory framework
ramy regulacyjne, system nadzoru
The system of rules and bodies that govern an industry or activity.
Critics argued that the existing regulatory framework was simply not designed to handle fraud on this scale.
whistleblower
sygnalista, osoba ujawniająca nieprawidłowości
A person who exposes wrongdoing within an organisation, often at personal risk.
It was a whistleblower inside the company who first alerted journalists to the scale of the contamination.
