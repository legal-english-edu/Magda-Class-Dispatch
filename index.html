Dispatch
dispatch/dɪˈspætʃ/
noun
1. A message or report sent quickly to its destination – especially one sent by a journalist from abroad, or between military officers.
2. The act of sending someone or something promptly to a particular place.

From the last lesson
When describing the limits of someone's official authority or responsibility, three words come up – and they are not interchangeable. Remit is the natural choice in British professional and institutional English. Competence (uncountable) carries the same meaning in formal legal English: this falls outside the competence of this court. Competences (plural) belongs specifically to EU institutional writing. Outside that context, the plural sounds like a translation.
