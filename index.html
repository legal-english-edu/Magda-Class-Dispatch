Dispatch
dispatch/dɪˈspætʃ/
noun
1. A message or report sent quickly to its destination – especially one sent by a journalist from abroad, or between military officers.
2. The act of sending someone or something promptly to a particular place.

From the last lesson
Three verbs came up in close succession – aggravate, exacerbate, wreak havoc. Worth keeping them separate in active use: exacerbate implies something already bad getting worse; aggravate is broader; wreak havoc suggests sweeping, chaotic destruction.
